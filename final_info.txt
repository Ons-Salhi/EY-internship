The challenge presented the question, How can we use machine learning to predict the world cup finals?

As participants, the challenge was to correctly predict Quarterfinals, Semifinals, Finals results and the World Cup Winner for the 2010, 2014, 2018 and 2022.

The data that was provided for us was as follows:
- A Dataset of FIFA Ranking 1992-2022
- A Dataset of Results of International Matches from 1872 to 2022 for both friendly and FIFA regulation matches.
- A Dataset of Results of games that reached Penalty Shoot-Outs from 1967 to 2022
- A Dataset of Team Groups starting from 2010 to 2022. Each World Cup had its own Dataset of Team distribution into Groups that make up the Group Stage.

Our approach was to analyze each Dataset separately, we found that in both the FIFA Ranking and International Matches Results, the Date column wasn't formatted correctly, so we fixed that.

In the International Matches Results Dataset, we chose to work only on FIFA regulation matches. So we filtered the Dataset by the feature "Tournament" and created a DataFrame subset of games that were not "Friendly".

Then we dropped some redundant features and grouped average points by year then we merged the International Matches Results Dataset with the FIFA Ranking Dataset by Year!

Finally, we applied 3 models to predict the results:
- Logistic Regression Classifier
- Random Forest Classifier
- LightGBM Classifier

We were able to correctly and consistently predict the 2010, 2014 and 2018 World Cup Winners, as well as Quarterfinal, Semifinal, and finals Teams. This netted us 24 points to win the challenge with the 2nd place team falling behind by 6 points.
(2018 results were not counted as they were obtained as the challenge results were being announced)
